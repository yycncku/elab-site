Entrepreneurship: The Lab

Where ventures reshape industries.

We are a group of researchers at National Cheng Kung University exploring how entrepreneurs' venturing efforts change the course of industrial dynamics — and drive economic evolution.

We believe the entrepreneur is the engine of economic evolution — so we study the people who leap, the communities that create, and the curiosity that keeps organizations alive.

Our research philosophy

Research directions

Three questions we keep asking.

01

Entrepreneurship

Why do people leap? We study the psychological side of venturing — from entrepreneurial intention and fear of failure to lean-startup practice and how entrepreneurship should be taught.

PsychologyLean StartupEducation
02

User Innovation

Innovation doesn't only come from firms. We trace how makers, open communities and user ecosystems create, share and commercialize ideas outside traditional R&D.

Maker MovementOpen CommunitiesEcosystems
03

Work Curiosity & Creativity

What keeps minds exploring at work? We examine curiosity, creative cognition and organizational designs that let employees—and organizations—stay inventive.

CuriosityCreative CognitionOrg Design
